## Body:

After 11 years at Microsoft, I left to do what I went there to do (technically their timing, but my choice). Microsoft kept me on for 60 days. Within those 60 days, I landed at Harper.

Total time "on the market": 30 days.

**That's not typical. That's privilege.**

The privilege I had:
‚Üí 30+ years of established reputation
‚Üí Extensive network
‚Üí Financial buffer to be selective
‚Üí Senior-level visibility
‚Üí Already-optimized LinkedIn profile

Most engineers don't have these advantages. They're grinding through applications, getting ghosted, wondering why their experience isn't translating to opportunities.

**But during my easy transition, I learned how the system actually works.**

As someone who's hired dozens of engineers AND just transitioned roles, I know what recruiters look for in those critical 10 seconds of scanning a profile.

**The pattern I saw:**

Strong engineers with weak GitHub profiles were invisible.

Not because they weren't talented. Because their profile didn't communicate their value in the time recruiters spend looking.

**The mismatch:**
- Engineer: 5 years building distributed systems
- GitHub: empty bio, list of repos, no context
- Recruiter: can't pattern-match to roles
- Result: no contact

**This isn't a skills problem. It's a visibility problem.**

**What actually matters (from the recruiter side):**

**1. LinkedIn ‚Üî GitHub integration**
Recruiters work across both platforms. Not connected = half your discoverability gone.
Impact: 3-5x more recruiter views when properly integrated.

**2. Clear value proposition**
Not: "Software engineer passionate about clean code"
Instead: "Backend Engineer, 8 years scaling distributed systems. Built pipeline handling 10M+ events/day. Python/Kafka/PostgreSQL. Led team of 4, reduced latency 60%."

Recruiters need to pattern-match in 3 seconds, not 3 minutes.

**3. Evidence of current activity**
Not "was good 5 years ago"
Show: contributions in last 3-6 months, updated repositories, current tech

Brilliant engineers are invisible with empty profiles. Mediocre engineers with great profiles get more opportunities.
