# PASTE INTO LINKEDIN

I went to Montana Festival because I recently started Buffalo Jump Forge, it is in startup mode. So I signed up: 2 days, 1,000 people, startup track. What I came home with is much different than what I went to find.

While I was there something broke free in my mind -- surfaced, breathing the air like a diver surfacing after a long swim. I felt truth flowing in a way I have missed for a very long time, from deep within my heart, delivering clarity to my mind.

During his talk, George Packer referenced his "Four Americas" piece — four ways of being American that don't talk to each other. What stopped me was his description of 'Just America' being organized around *grievance*. The moral engine is a catalogue of wrongs. I've been trying to name something since 2016 and that frame broke something free in the stalemate of my thinking.

Then Cristina Mormorunni and Kirk Johnson did a panel on bison and Indigenous land stewardship. Cristina screened a film she made of Yo-Yo Ma playing Amazing Grace as bison listen. The setting was the Blackfeet Nation — bison returning home to rejoin their brothers and sisters after more than a century.

Together with Packer's frame, that image finished a thought I'd been working on for a long time.

We're running on the urgency of fear — fear of what we're losing, fear of what's coming. It energizes, fear mobilizes, but it doesn't build anything; eventually it destroys us, separates, isolates, terrifies, and paralyzes us. We have to move from the urgency of fear to the urgency of hope — not just a belief the future will be better, but an intentional investment in making the future better for everyone, working together in community, as a family. e pluribus unum, "A Many One". 

It requires having something to invite people into, so I am inviting you into this conversation, this moment, this shift — from fear to hope.

Buffalo Jump Ranch is 150+ acres in the lower Madison Valley, 45 minutes west of Bozeman, and it's where Ann Snowberger and I are building toward that. The Ranch is a place to retreat into the vast quiet of rural Montana — not metaphorically, literally — a place to stop and be somewhere, where the valley is big and the noise falls away faster than you'd expect. The elk in the hills, the blackbirds in the field, the fish in the Madison create the community and welcome us to remember who we are, that we are created to create, and that we exist in community with the earth and all living things.

I went to the festival to figure out how to build Buffalo Jump Forge. I came back knowing why we're building Buffalo Jump Ranch.

None of this happens without Yarrow Kraner, Padden Guy Murphy, and Lumay Wang Murphy and the Last Best Future team creating the conditions for 1,000 people to think together. Thank you for building Montana Festival. (Links in comments.)
