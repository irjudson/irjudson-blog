Went to Montana Festival last week. Two days in Bozeman. Lost my voice — worth it.

George Packer was there. During his talk, he referenced his "Four Americas" piece. The part that stopped me: "Just America," the progressive left, is organized around *grievance* rather than aspiration. I've been trying to name something since 2016 and that was most of it.

Then Cristina Mormorunni and Kirk Johnson on bison and Indigenous land stewardship. Cristina screened a film she made of Yo-Yo Ma playing Amazing Grace as bison were returned to the Blackfeet Nation — coming home to their brother and sister after more than a century.

The thing I've been trying to name: we're running on the urgency of fear. We need the urgency of hope instead. They're not the same thing. (Links in comments.)

Buffalo Jump Ranch — 150 acres in the lower Madison Valley, 45 minutes west of Bozeman — is where Buffalo Jump Forge is built. It's also a place for people who want to do that work. The vast quiet of rural Montana. Workshops where nobody is the expert and everyone is a student. The elk and the birds and the fish aren't decoration — they're part of the community.

I went to the festival to build business. That happened. I came back knowing what I'm building it for.
